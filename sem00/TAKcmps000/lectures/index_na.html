To print these notes out in the most economical way:

  1. Click to select desired lecture, above.
  2. On the starting information screen, click on "Click here to Start" to enter the framed slide viewer. Although dimensions of the frames may differ, depending on your browser (and they can also be moved by dragging the borders), it should resemble the following example:
  3. Click on "Expand" in frame A. This will expand the topics in frame B to show details.
  4. Click inside frame B to make it the selected frame. (Unless you click on a link, you will probably not notice any change.)
  5. From the file menu, choose "Print Frame" and finish the print dialog box to start printing.
These instructions work as described in Netscape Navigator (Internet Explorer should work in a similar fashion) to print the textual part of the slides. Should you need to print a graphic, you should click in frame D before choosing "Print Frame", but remember that this takes a great deal of ink or toner, and for text will not give as good a result.

By the way, frame C is used for navigating among the graphical slides, while frame E is for notes that may accompany each slide (I do not usually provide these).
